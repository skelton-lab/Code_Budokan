# History of Computing — Babbage to the Transformer

**Source:** *Code Budokan: A History of Computer Science & Programming* (Historical Foundations Series, prepared for Anthony Skelton, June 2026) — converted in full from its original `.docx` into the eight linkable markdown files in this directory. Nothing has been summarized or cut: every biographical profile, milestone table, reading-list entry, and the biographical index appear here in full, restructured for the web but not condensed.

This is the companion layer's own deep background. Where the companion overview and the 28 per-language files link *into* specific guide sections, these nine files are the connected narrative underneath all of them — the people, machines, and papers that make any individual language's founding paper legible as a response to something, not an arrival from nowhere.

## The seven eras

| Era | Years | File | Key figures |
|---|---|---|---|
| I | 1820–1930 | [`era-1-mathematical-foundations.md`](era-1-mathematical-foundations.md) | Babbage, Lovelace, Boole, Frege & Russell |
| II | 1930–1945 | [`era-2-theoretical-computer.md`](era-2-theoretical-computer.md) | Turing, Church, Shannon |
| III | 1945–1959 | [`era-3-electronic-computer.md`](era-3-electronic-computer.md) | von Neumann, Hopper, Backus |
| IV | 1960–1979 | [`era-4-software-revolution.md`](era-4-software-revolution.md) | Dijkstra, Thompson & Ritchie, Knuth, Codd |
| V | 1975–1989 | [`era-5-personal-computer.md`](era-5-personal-computer.md) | Wozniak & Jobs, Gates & Allen |
| VI | 1990–2005 | [`era-6-internet-and-web.md`](era-6-internet-and-web.md) | Berners-Lee, Torvalds, Page & Brin |
| VII | 2006–2017 | [`era-7-deep-learning.md`](era-7-deep-learning.md) | Hinton, LeCun, and the road to the Transformer |

Two reference files sit alongside the seven eras:

- [`reading-list.md`](reading-list.md) — the 24 primary-source papers the eras above draw on, organized by era, in the order the Code Budokan reading programme prioritizes them.
- [`biographical-index.md`](biographical-index.md) — a single chronological table of every figure profiled across all seven eras, by birth year.

## How this connects to the language guides

Twenty of the 28 `code-rookie` guides now link into these era files directly from their own companion file's historical note — [`fortran.md`](../fortran.md) into Era III's Backus profile, [`cobol.md`](../cobol.md) into Era III's Hopper profile, [`sql.md`](../sql.md) into Era IV's Codd profile, [`c.md`](../c.md) and [`go.md`](../go.md) into Era IV's Thompson & Ritchie profile, [`python.md`](../python.md) into Era VII, and so on. Each era file also links back out to the companion files it connects to, in its own "In this companion" section — the linking runs both directions.
