# Reading List — Key Papers in the History of Computing

The following papers and works are the primary sources for the history documented across the seven era files in this directory. They are organized by era and prioritized for the Code Budokan reading programme.

## Era I–II: Foundations

**[1]** *Turing, A. M. (1936). On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem.*
> Proceedings of the London Mathematical Society, 42(1), 230–265. The foundational paper of computer science. Defines the Turing Machine and proves the halting problem. See [Era II](era-2-theoretical-computer.md).

**[2]** *Shannon, C. E. (1937). A Symbolic Analysis of Relay and Switching Circuits.*
> MIT Master's Thesis. Proves Boolean algebra describes switching circuits. Foundation of digital electronics. See [Era II](era-2-theoretical-computer.md).

**[3]** *Shannon, C. E. (1948). A Mathematical Theory of Communication.*
> Bell System Technical Journal, 27, 379–423. Founds information theory. Defines the bit, entropy, channel capacity. See [Era II](era-2-theoretical-computer.md).

**[4]** *Turing, A. M. (1950). Computing Machinery and Intelligence.*
> Mind, 59(236), 433–460. Proposes the Turing Test. First serious discussion of machine intelligence. See [Era II](era-2-theoretical-computer.md).

**[5]** *Church, A. (1936). An unsolvable problem of elementary number theory.*
> American Journal of Mathematics, 58(2), 345–363. Lambda calculus and the Church-Turing Thesis. See [Era II](era-2-theoretical-computer.md).

## Era III: The Electronic Computer

**[6]** *von Neumann, J. (1945). First Draft of a Report on the EDVAC.*
> University of Pennsylvania. Defines the stored-program architecture of all modern computers. See [Era III](era-3-electronic-computer.md).

**[7]** *Backus, J. W., et al. (1957). The FORTRAN Automatic Coding System.*
> Proceedings of the Western Joint Computer Conference. First high-level compiled language. See [Era III](era-3-electronic-computer.md) and [`../fortran.md`](../fortran.md).

**[8]** *McCarthy, J. (1960). Recursive Functions of Symbolic Expressions and Their Computation by Machine.*
> Communications of the ACM, 3(4), 184–195. The Lisp paper. Defines functional programming foundations. See [Era III](era-3-electronic-computer.md) and [`../scheme.md`](../scheme.md).

**[9]** *Hopper, G. (1952). The Education of a Computer.*
> Proceedings of the 1952 ACM Annual Meeting. First description of a compiler. See [Era III](era-3-electronic-computer.md) and [`../cobol.md`](../cobol.md).

## Era IV: The Software Revolution

**[10]** *Dijkstra, E. W. (1968). Go To Statement Considered Harmful.*
> Communications of the ACM, 11(3), 147–148. Launches the structured programming movement. See [Era IV](era-4-software-revolution.md) and [`../algol.md`](../algol.md).

**[11]** *Ritchie, D. M., & Thompson, K. (1974). The UNIX Time-Sharing System.*
> Communications of the ACM, 17(7), 365–375. Defines Unix design philosophy. See [Era IV](era-4-software-revolution.md) and [`../c.md`](../c.md).

**[12]** *Codd, E. F. (1970). A Relational Model of Data for Large Shared Data Banks.*
> Communications of the ACM, 13(6), 377–387. Foundation of all relational databases. See [Era IV](era-4-software-revolution.md) and [`../sql.md`](../sql.md).

**[13]** *Kernighan, B. W., & Ritchie, D. M. (1978). The C Programming Language.*
> Prentice Hall. K&R — the definitive C language reference. See [Era IV](era-4-software-revolution.md) and [`../c.md`](../c.md).

**[14]** *Cerf, V., & Kahn, R. (1974). A Protocol for Packet Network Interconnection.*
> IEEE Transactions on Communications, 22(5), 637–648. TCP/IP. Foundation of the internet. See [Era IV](era-4-software-revolution.md).

## Era VI: The Web and Open Source

**[15]** *Berners-Lee, T. (1989). Information Management: A Proposal.*
> CERN Internal Memo. The original WWW proposal. HTTP, HTML, URLs conceived. See [Era VI](era-6-internet-and-web.md).

**[16]** *Brin, S., & Page, L. (1998). The Anatomy of a Large-Scale Hypertextual Web Search Engine.*
> Proceedings of the 7th International Conference on World Wide Web. Google's founding paper. PageRank algorithm. See [Era VI](era-6-internet-and-web.md).

**[17]** *Raymond, E. S. (1999). The Cathedral and the Bazaar.*
> O'Reilly. The philosophical manifesto of the open-source movement. See [Era VI](era-6-internet-and-web.md).

## Era VII: Deep Learning

**[18]** *Rumelhart, D. E., Hinton, G. E., & Williams, R. J. (1986). Learning representations by back-propagating errors.*
> Nature, 323, 533–536. Backpropagation for multi-layer neural networks. See [Era VII](era-7-deep-learning.md) and [`../python.md`](../python.md).

**[19]** *LeCun, Y., Bottou, L., Bengio, Y., & Haffner, P. (1998). Gradient-based learning applied to document recognition.*
> Proceedings of the IEEE, 86(11), 2278–2324. CNNs for visual recognition. LeNet-5. See [Era VII](era-7-deep-learning.md).

**[20]** *Krizhevsky, A., Sutskever, I., & Hinton, G. (2012). ImageNet Classification with Deep Convolutional Neural Networks.*
> NeurIPS 2012. AlexNet paper. Launches the modern deep learning era. See [Era VII](era-7-deep-learning.md).

**[21]** *Mikolov, T., et al. (2013). Distributed Representations of Words and Phrases and their Compositionality.*
> NeurIPS 2013. Word2Vec. Foundation of word embeddings and NLP representation learning. See [Era VII](era-7-deep-learning.md).

**[22]** *Goodfellow, I., et al. (2014). Generative Adversarial Nets.*
> NeurIPS 2014. GANs. Foundation of generative AI for images. See [Era VII](era-7-deep-learning.md).

**[23]** *Bahdanau, D., Cho, K., & Bengio, Y. (2014). Neural Machine Translation by Jointly Learning to Align and Translate.*
> arXiv:1409.0473. The attention mechanism. Immediate predecessor of the transformer. See [Era VII § The road to the Transformer](era-7-deep-learning.md#the-road-to-the-transformer).

**[24]** *Vaswani, A., et al. (2017). Attention Is All You Need.*
> NeurIPS 2017. arXiv:1706.03762. The transformer. The paper that made GPT, BERT, Claude, and Gemini possible. End of this document; beginning of [`../../appendix-ai-orchestration/00-overview.md`](../../appendix-ai-orchestration/00-overview.md).
